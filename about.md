## About Me

For as long as I can remember, I have been deeply passionate about the natural world.
Today, that lifelong connection has evolved into a dedicated commitment to nature conservation, preservation, and ecological restoration.
Driven by the vision of a future where humanity **"lives in harmony with Nature"**, I see my research as a way to contribute actively to achieving the global 2030 sustainability goals.

### Academic Journey & Research Focus

I hold a degree in Forestry Science, which culminated in a Master’s thesis exploring the multi-dimensional facets of Sustainable Forest Management (SFM) within Italian Protected Areas.
This research allowed me to analyze sustainability across all its critical layers: ecological, economic, social, and cultural. 

Currently, as a PhD student, my work centers on monitoring the management effectiveness of Protected Areas. 
By leveraging cutting-edge remote and proximal sensing indicators, my project compares how strictly protected forest ecosystems respond to climate stressors relative to unprotected stands. 

Beyond core research, I have developed strong skills in project management, with a specific focus on navigating European funding instruments for scientific projects.

### International Experience

International collaboration and diverse environments have heavily shaped my academic background:
* **University of Huelva, Spain:** During my Bachelor’s degree, I spent five months as an Erasmus student gaining valuable fieldwork experience. I later returned to the same institution for a post-graduate traineeship specialized in remote sensing.
* **Freie Universität (FU) Berlin, Germany:** I have spent 5 months at FU as guest research, further expanding my technical toolkit and international network.
* **Universität für Bodenkultur (BOKU) Vienna, Austria:** I am currently undertaking another research training period in BOKU for fully automatize my pipeline.

My connection to nature extends well beyond academic research into practical, hands-on experiences in rural environments:
* **Agroecology:** With my brother  I have been managing an organic vegetable garden utilizing ecological farming methods. To ground these practices, I completed a specialized training course to become a technician in social and multifunctional agriculture.
* **Trekking:** When I am not analyzing geospatial data, I am likely out exploring trails. I am an avid trekker and have completed three long-distance routes across Italy, including the historic *“Path of the Gods”* (Via degli Dei) connecting Bologna to Florence.
